Concept Mobile App End-to-End

Simplifying Appointment Booking for Solo Pet Groomers

Solo pet groomers run their entire business from their phones — but the tools built for them were designed for multi-location operations they'll never need. I conceived and built PawBook end-to-end: a mobile booking app that replaces WhatsApp and a paper diary with automated scheduling, SMS reminders, and client history — all completable in under a minute per booking. From PRD to shipped front end, this was a fully AI-augmented solo build.

Product Designer · Mobile UI/UX · AI UX

Problem

Solo groomers lose revenue to tools built for someone else

Solo groomers lose real revenue to no-shows, double bookings, and back-and-forth confirmation messages. The tools that exist were built for bigger operations — too complex, too expensive, and too much to learn between grooming appointments.

The primary persona, Sam, tried two apps and went back to paper. That's not a preference — that's a product failure. The actual need was simple: something that handles booking and client communication automatically, so she never has to chase a no-show again.

The design challenge wasn't features. It was trust. Sam uses her phone one-handed, outdoors, between appointments. Any friction in the flow is friction she won't tolerate twice.

Goals

What success looked like

  • First booking completable within 10 minutes of install
  • Reduce no-show rate by 40% within 30 days through automated SMS reminders
  • Keep daily booking communication time under 10 minutes
  • Replace WhatsApp and paper diary with one tool that runs itself
Research

Three findings shaped the product

  • Groomers who abandoned software cited complexity and setup time as the reason — not missing features
  • No-shows and last-minute cancellations were the single most cited revenue problem, and most groomers were still chasing confirmations manually via WhatsApp
  • One-handed outdoor mobile use was a non-negotiable constraint — not a nice-to-have accessibility note

These findings drove the core product bet: automate the communication entirely, and make the booking flow fast enough that it never feels like software.

Strategy

Speed and automation over features

Every design decision was filtered through one question: does this make Sam faster, or does it make her think?

I designed two booking paths — manual in-app creation for walk-ins and phone calls, and an automated WhatsApp bot that captures bookings from clients directly. Both paths feed into the same system: the groomer gets a push notification, approves or declines, and the booking lands on the calendar. No double-entry. No switching between apps.

Navigation was kept to four tabs — Today, Calendar, Clients, Settings — because Sam checks her schedule first thing every morning and last thing every night. The dashboard needed to be the first thing she sees, not a home screen she has to navigate away from.

Decisions

Five decisions that defined the product

Today dashboard as a daily assistant

The Today screen isn't a generic home page — it's a morning briefing. At a glance: how many booked today, how many confirmed, how many cancelled, and a scrollable list of who's coming in. Sam opens the app once in the morning and knows her entire day. The trade-off: less screen space for other features on the primary view, but this is the screen that gets opened 10x a day — it earns the real estate.

Dual booking paths — fast manual entry + WhatsApp bot automation

Groomers receive bookings two ways: clients message on WhatsApp, or the groomer takes one over the phone. The manual in-app flow makes booking creation faster than writing it down. The WhatsApp bot handles the other path — it captures client details, suggests available slots, and sends the request as a notification. The trade-off: two entry points means two flows to maintain, but it matches how groomers actually receive bookings — and both paths eliminate double-booking by design.

Approval gate before calendar entry

Bookings from the WhatsApp bot don't auto-land on the calendar. They arrive as pending requests with a notification. The groomer reviews, approves, or declines. This keeps the groomer in control — no surprise bookings, no conflicts they didn't agree to. The trade-off: one extra tap per booking, but it builds trust that the calendar is always accurate.

Automated follow-ups at 24 hours and 2 hours before schedule

Reminders fire automatically — 24 hours before for planning, 2 hours before as a final nudge. The groomer doesn't configure, trigger, or think about them. Twilio handles delivery. If an SMS fails, the app surfaces a clear notification so the groomer can follow up manually. The trade-off: SMS costs per booking, but the 40% no-show reduction target makes the ROI obvious.

Core Flow

Two paths, one calendar

Path A — Manual Booking (in-app)

Dashboard → Tap FAB to open new booking
Search or add client
Select or add pet — auto-populated from client record
Select service via pill selector
Select date, then available time slot
Tap confirm → booking added to calendar, SMS confirmation sent to client

Path B — WhatsApp Bot Booking (automated)

Client messages groomer's WhatsApp business number
Bot captures client name, pet, preferred service, and date/time
Bot suggests available slots based on calendar
Client confirms preferred slot
Push notification sent to groomer's app → "New booking request"
Groomer reviews details → taps Approve or Decline
Approved → booking lands on calendar, client gets WhatsApp confirmation
Declined → client gets message with alternative slots

After booking (both paths)

  • 24-hour reminder fires automatically via SMS
  • 2-hour reminder fires automatically via SMS
  • Failure states surface clearly — never a silent failure
Solution

A React Native app built end-to-end, solo

A React Native mobile app built with Expo, Supabase, and NativeWind — conceived, designed, and vibe-coded solo. The system handles the full grooming business workflow through two booking paths (manual + WhatsApp bot), an approval gate, a daily assistant dashboard, automated follow-ups, client profiles with no-show tracking, and calendar management. The entire system is designed around one constraint: Sam shouldn't have to think about it.

Outcome

Full flow shipped — validation pending

Full end-to-end flow from onboarding to booking to automated follow-up designed and shipped as a working concept. Success metrics were defined upfront and tied to specific screens — setup completion within 10 minutes, 40% no-show reduction, and under 10 minutes of daily booking communication time.

Quantitative validation is pending live user testing. The primary assumption to validate first: whether the dual-path booking model (manual + WhatsApp bot) reduces total groomer workload compared to a single manual flow.

Reflection

What I'd do differently and what's next

  • Run usability testing on the WhatsApp bot flow earlier — it's the most complex system interaction and the highest trust risk
  • If the bot misinterprets a client message, the groomer loses confidence in the entire system. Identifying that risk in the PRD was right; validating it before building would have been better
  • Next priority: a client self-booking link that bypasses WhatsApp entirely — the single highest-leverage feature for reducing groomer workload further
  • It's a deliberate v1 exclusion, but the data model is already built to support it
